About Me

Hi! I'm Ana, and I am a current senior undergraduate student at the University of Wisconsin–Madison. I am double majoring in Geology & Geophysics and Cartography & GIS.

As a geology student, I am currently working at UW–Madison’s WiscAr Lab on a senior thesis dating pseudotachylytes from the Main Central Thrust Fault in the Himalayas. Through this work, we aim to better understand the seismic history of this tectonically active region. I have a wide range of interests within geology, including petrology, geochemistry, and volcanism, as well as work that takes a more environmental direction, such as environmental remediation and hydrogeology. You can learn more about my background and experience.

Through my second major in Cartography and GIS, I have taken courses that allowed me to build strong skills in GIS alongside programming tools such as Python, which I use for data analysis and visualization. These skills have been especially useful in internships and research projects where spatial analysis and clear visual communication were essential. My training in cartography has also allowed me to practice mapmaking as a form of storytelling, using tools such as Illustrator, Photoshop, and ArcGIS StoryMaps to communicate environmental topics in ways that center both place and community.

In my free time, I enjoy spending time outdoors and hanging out with my cats. You can reach me through my email or connect with me on Linkedin!
